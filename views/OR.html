CRITICAL PATH METHOD EXAMPLE

Critical Path Method, abbreviated as CPM, is a network technique that is concerned with obtaining the trade-off, between cost and completion date for large projects. In any project consisting of several activities each activity can be completed in a normal duration with normal cost. If we employ more persons or skilled people or given overtime to the workers, the activity could be completed in a reduced duration known as crash duration. But this involves an increased cost in the form of additional resources. With CPM the amount needed to complete the various activities is assumed to be known with certainity. So, the direct costs for the activities increase and hence the cost of the project also increases. By reducing the activity duartion of some or all possible activities, the project completion time is decreased or the project is completed ahead of the schedule. This will naturally reduce the overhead cost for the entire project. On one hand the direct expenses increase, if we shorten the activity duration, but, the indirect expenses for the project are reduced. We have to strike a balance or an optimum time schedule, or a least cost schedule is to be obtained. This is the purpose of the Critical Path Method.

Applications where its found useful:

  • Construction of a building or of a highway.
  • Planning and launching a new product.
  • Scheduling maintenance for a project.
  • Manufacture and assembly of a large machine tool
  • Conduct a music or drama festival.
  • Preparation of budget for a company.
